Mizutaki

(Japanese: 水炊き) Mizutaki is a traditional Japanese hot pot dish made mainly with chicken simmered in a light broth. It originated in the Fukuoka region of Japan and is known for its simple ingredients and delicate flavor. The dish is usually cooked at the table and eaten by dipping the cooked ingredients into ponzu sauce.

Key Characteristics

  • Base ingredients:Chicken, napa cabbage, tofu, mushrooms, and other vegetables.
  • Broth: A clear chicken broth created by slowly simmering chicken
  • Dipping sauce: Typically served with ponzu sauce.
  • Cooking style: Prepared as a hot pot where ingredients are cooked together in one pot.

How to Make Mizutaki:

  • Simmer chicken in water to create a light broth.
  • Add vegetables and tofu to the pot.
  • Cook ingredients together and dip them in ponzu sauce before eating.

Popular Recipes

Yakitori (grilled chicken) Tsukune (minced meat patties) Okonomiyaki (savory pancakes) Mizutaki (chicken stew)
