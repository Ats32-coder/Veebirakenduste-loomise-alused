Tsukune

(Japanese: つくね) is a Japanese-style minced meat skewer, usually made from chicken, and commonly served at yakitori restaurants or in izakaya bars. Unlike regular meatballs, tsukune has a softer texture and is typically seasoned with a distinctive sweet-salty glaze.



Key Characteristics

  • Main ingredient: Most often made from ground chicken (especially thigh meat for extra juiciness), though pork, fish, or even shrimp may sometimes be used.
  • Texture: Finely chopped chicken cartilage is often mixed in to add a pleasant crunchy element.
  • Sauce (Tare): The cooked skewers are brushed with a thick, glossy tare sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar.
  • Serving style: Traditionally grilled over charcoal, though they can also be pan-fried at home. They are often served with a raw egg yolk on the side, into which the skewer is dipped before eating to mellow and enrich the flavor.

How to Make Tsukune:

  • Mixing: Combine the ground meat with grated ginger, chopped green onion, an egg, and a small amount of starch or panko breadcrumbs to bind the mixture.
  • Shaping: Form the mixture into oval or round balls and shape them onto bamboo skewers.
  • Cooking: Grill or pan-fry the skewers until golden brown and cooked through.
  • Glazing: In the final stage of cooking, dip or brush the skewers with tare sauce and cook until the glaze slightly caramelizes.
